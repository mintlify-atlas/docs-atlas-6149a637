Aria's AI capabilities transform how doctors create prescriptions and access patient information. Our intelligent features recognize medical entities in real-time, provide instant medication lookups, and enable natural language queries about patient history.

## Medical Voice Dictation

Speak naturally to create prescriptions while our AI automatically identifies and highlights medical entities as you dictate.

### Real-Time Entity Recognition

As you speak, Aria's AI engine detects and categorizes:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Medications" icon="pill">
    Automatically identifies drug names with dosages (e.g., "Paracetamol 500mg", "Cetirizine 10mg")
  </Card>
  <Card title="Symptoms" icon="activity">
    Recognizes patient complaints and symptoms (e.g., "fever", "headache", "body aches")
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start Dictation">
    Tap the microphone icon to begin voice input. Speak naturally about the patient's condition and treatment plan.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Real-Time Processing">
    Our AI processes your speech in real-time, transcribing and highlighting medical entities as you speak.
    
    Example dictation:
    ```
    "Patient complains of fever and headache for 3 days. 
    Prescribing Paracetamol 500mg and Cetirizine 10mg for symptom relief."
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Automatic Classification">
    Detected entities are automatically categorized and displayed:
    - **Symptoms**: fever, headache (highlighted in amber)
    - **Medications**: Paracetamol 500mg, Cetirizine 10mg (highlighted with border)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review and Confirm">
    Review the transcribed text with highlighted entities. Edit if needed, then add to prescription.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The smart dictation feature supports medical terminology in English and recognizes common Indian medication brands.
</Note>

## AI-Aided Diagnosis

Get intelligent diagnosis suggestions based on patient complaints AND complete medical history.

### Context-Aware Recommendations

Unlike simple symptom checkers, Aria's AI cross-references:

- Current chief complaints
- Patient's previous prescriptions
- Lab report history
- Existing conditions and allergies
- Seasonal patterns and trends

### Diagnosis Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter Complaints">
    Type or dictate the patient's chief complaints:
    ```
    Fever (101°F), severe headache, body aches, fatigue for 3 days
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="AI Analysis">
    Aria analyzes the complaints along with the patient's complete medical history from ABHA-linked records.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Suggestions">
    Receive ranked diagnosis suggestions with confidence scores:
    
    **Viral Fever (URI)** - 87% confidence
    - Symptoms align with recent prescription history (Paracetamol, Dec 15)
    - Seasonal pattern noted in patient records
    
    **Influenza Type B** - 68% confidence  
    - High fever with body aches present
    - No recent flu vaccination in records
    - Consider PCR test for confirmation
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Informed Decision">
    Use AI suggestions as a clinical decision support tool. Final diagnosis remains with the physician.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
AI diagnosis suggestions are meant to assist clinical decision-making, not replace physician judgment. Always verify suggestions with clinical examination and appropriate diagnostic tests.
</Warning>

## Patient Health Assistant (RAG-Powered)

Ask questions about any patient in natural language and get instant answers from their complete medical history.

### RAG Technology

Aria uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to:

1. Retrieve relevant information from patient records
2. Generate accurate, contextual answers
3. Provide source citations for verification

### Example Queries

  <Card title="Health Summary">

Previous history includes seasonal allergies. No chronic conditions. Overall healthy profile with occasional viral infections."
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Allergy Check">
    **Query**: "Any drug allergies?"

  <Card title="Medication History">

  </Card>

### Use Cases

- Quick allergy checks before prescribing
- Review previous treatment responses
- Identify medication patterns
- Verify patient-reported medical history
- Prepare for patient consultations

<Info>

</Info>
