Aria's digital prescription system revolutionizes how doctors create and patients access prescriptions. Built on ABHA standards, every prescription is permanently linked to the patient's medical record and accessible anytime, anywhere.

## ABHA Integration

Aria is fully integrated with India's Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) infrastructure.

### What is ABHA?

ABHA (Ayushman Bharat Health Account) is a unique 14-digit health ID that enables:

- **Unified Medical Records**: All prescriptions and records linked to one ID
- **Interoperability**: Share records across healthcare providers
- **Patient Control**: Patients own and control their health data
- **Lifetime Access**: Permanent medical record that never gets lost

<Info>
Learn more about ABHA at [abha.abdm.gov.in](https://abha.abdm.gov.in/abha/v3)
</Info>

## Creating Prescriptions

Aria offers multiple ways to create prescriptions efficiently.

### Voice Dictation Method

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Patient">
    Choose the patient from your list or search by ABHA ID/name.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Activate Voice Input">
    Tap the microphone icon to start dictation mode.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Speak Naturally">
    Dictate the prescription details:
    ```
    "Patient complains of fever and headache for 3 days. 
    Prescribing Paracetamol 500mg and Cetirizine 10mg for symptom relief."
    ```
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Detected Entities">
    AI automatically highlights:
    - Symptoms: fever, headache
    - Medications: Paracetamol 500mg, Cetirizine 10mg
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Prescription Details">
    Specify for each medication:
    - Dosage frequency (e.g., "twice daily")
    - Duration (e.g., "5 days")
    - Instructions (e.g., "after meals")
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and Share">
    Save the prescription. Patient instantly receives it via their ABHA-linked app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Manual Entry Method

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search Medication">
    Use the smart search to find medications:
    - Type partial name (e.g., "para" for Paracetamol)
    - Select from dosage variants
    - View composition and alternatives
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Dosage">
    Set medication details:
    - Quantity per dose
    - Frequency (OD/BD/TDS/QID or custom)
    - Route (oral/topical/injection)
    - Duration
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Diagnosis">
    Enter the diagnosis or chief complaint for the prescription.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Include Notes">
    Add any special instructions or follow-up recommendations.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Finalize">
    Review and save. The prescription is automatically linked to the patient's ABHA record.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Prescription Components

Each digital prescription in Aria includes:

### Patient Information
- ABHA ID
- Name, age, gender
- Contact information
- Known allergies and conditions

### Clinical Information
- Chief complaints/symptoms
- Diagnosis (with ICD-10 code support)
- Vital signs (if recorded)
- Clinical notes

### Medication Details
For each prescribed medication:

```json
{
  "medication": "Paracetamol 500mg",
  "composition": "Paracetamol",
  "dosage": "1 tablet",
  "frequency": "Twice daily",
  "timing": "After meals",
  "duration": "5 days",
  "route": "Oral"
}
```

### Doctor Information
- Doctor's name and registration number
- Clinic/hospital details
- Digital signature
- Prescription date and time

<Note>
All prescriptions are timestamped and include a unique prescription ID for tracking and verification.
</Note>

## Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify Patient Identity">
    Always confirm the patient's ABHA ID before creating a prescription to ensure correct record linkage.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check Allergies">
    Review patient allergies before prescribing. Use the RAG assistant to quickly check: "Any drug allergies?"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete All Fields">
    Include diagnosis, dosage instructions, and duration for comprehensive patient records.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Use Standard Terminology">
    Use standard medical terminology and ICD-10 codes when available for better interoperability.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Regular Follow-ups">
    Schedule and document follow-up appointments in the prescription notes.
  </Step>
</Steps>
