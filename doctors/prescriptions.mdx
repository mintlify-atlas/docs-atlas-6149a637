## Digital Prescription Workflow

Create complete digital prescriptions that are automatically linked to the patient's ABHA account and accessible forever.

<Info>
  Every prescription you create is ABDM (Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission) compliant and automatically synced with the national health stack.
</Info>

## Creating a Prescription

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select patient">
    Search for patient by name, phone number, or ABHA ID
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review patient history">
    Check complete medical timeline for context (allergies, chronic conditions, recent medications)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter diagnosis">
    Type or dictate the diagnosis and chief complaints
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add medications">
    Use Smart Medication Search or voice dictation to add prescribed drugs
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify dosages & duration">
    Set dosage, frequency, timing, and treatment duration for each medication
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add instructions">
    Include additional notes, lifestyle advice, or follow-up instructions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send prescription">
    Digital prescription is sent to patient's ABHA account and mobile app instantly
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Dosage & Frequency Settings

For each medication, specify:

### Dosage Amount
- Number of tablets/capsules/ml per dose
- Examples: "1 tablet", "2 capsules", "5ml"

### Frequency
<Accordion title="Common Frequency Options">
  - **OD (Once Daily)**: One time per day
  - **BD (Twice Daily)**: Two times per day
  - **TDS (Three Times Daily)**: Three times per day
  - **QID (Four Times Daily)**: Four times per day
  - **SOS (As Needed)**: When required
  - **HS (Before Sleep)**: At bedtime
  - **Custom**: Specify your own schedule
</Accordion>

### Timing
- Before food
- After food
- With food
- Empty stomach
- Anytime

### Duration
- Number of days (e.g., "5 days", "14 days")
- Or "Continue until follow-up"
- Or "As directed"

<Warning>
  Always specify clear duration to prevent over-use or under-use of medications. Patients rely on these instructions.
</Warning>

## Using Voice Dictation for Prescriptions

Speed up prescription creation with voice:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open voice dictation">
    Click microphone icon in the prescription form
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dictate naturally">
    Say: "Patient complains of fever and headache for 3 days. Prescribing Paracetamol 500mg twice daily and Cetirizine 10mg once daily for 5 days."
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI processes">
    - Complaints extracted: Fever, headache (3 days)
    - Medications detected: Paracetamol 500mg, Cetirizine 10mg
    - Dosages recognized: Twice daily, once daily
    - Duration extracted: 5 days
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and confirm">
    Check detected entities and finalize prescription
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Voice dictation is particularly useful during patient consultations when you want to document while maintaining eye contact with the patient.
</Info>

## Drug Safety Checks

Automated safety checks run before finalizing prescriptions:

<Warning>
  **Automatic Alerts:**
  
  1. **Drug Allergies**: System checks patient's allergy list
     - Blocks prescription if allergic medication detected
     - Shows warning for medications in same class
  
  2. **Drug-Drug Interactions**:
     - Warns about dangerous combinations
     - Shows interaction severity (major, moderate, minor)
     - Suggests alternative medications
  
  3. **Duplicate Therapy**:
     - Alerts if patient already has active prescription for same medication
     - Shows prescribing doctor and date
  
  4. **Contraindications**:
     - Checks against patient's chronic conditions
     - Warns about medications unsafe for specific conditions (e.g., NSAIDs with kidney disease)
</Warning>

### Example Safety Alert

<Accordion title="Drug Allergy Alert">
  **⚠️ ALLERGY DETECTED**
  
  Cannot prescribe **Amoxicillin 500mg**
  
  Patient has documented allergy to:
  - **Penicillin** (Documented: Nov 2024)
  - Reaction: Severe rash and breathing difficulty
  
  **Suggested Alternatives:**
  - Azithromycin 500mg
  - Levofloxacin 500mg
  - Doxycycline 100mg
</Accordion>

## Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Always check allergies">
    Review patient allergy list before prescribing any new medication
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review active medications">
    Check what patient is currently taking to avoid duplicates and interactions
  </Step>
  <Step title="Specify clear durations">
    Never leave duration ambiguous - be specific about treatment length
  </Step>
  <Step title="Include follow-up">
    Always specify when patient should return for review
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use patient-friendly language">
    Write instructions patients can understand ("after breakfast" instead of "post-prandial")
  </Step>
</Steps>
