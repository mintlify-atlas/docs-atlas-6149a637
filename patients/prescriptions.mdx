## Your Prescriptions, Digitally

Every time your doctor creates a prescription using Aria, it automatically appears in your patient account. No more lost paper prescriptions, illegible handwriting, or forgotten medication instructions.

<Info>
  Digital prescriptions in Aria are ABHA-linked and accepted by pharmacies across India. They contain all the information you need to fill your prescriptions and take medications correctly.
</Info>

## How Digital Prescriptions Work

### Automatic Synchronization

When your doctor creates a prescription in the Aria Doctor app:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Doctor Creates Prescription">
    Your physician uses voice dictation or smart medication search to create your prescription during the consultation
  </Step>
  <Step title="Instant Delivery">
    The prescription is instantly saved to your Aria patient account and linked to your ABHA record
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notification">
    You receive a push notification that a new prescription is available
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access Anytime">
    View the complete prescription details in your app immediately
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **No Manual Entry Required** - Prescriptions are automatically added to your account. You don't need to scan, photograph, or manually enter any information.
</Note>

## Viewing Your Prescriptions

All your prescriptions are organized in your medical timeline and easily accessible.

### Access Your Prescriptions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Aria App">
    Launch the Aria patient app on your mobile device
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to Prescriptions">
    Tap **Prescriptions** from the main menu, or view them in your **Medical Timeline**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse by Date">
    Prescriptions are displayed in reverse chronological order, with the newest first
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap to View Details">
    Click on any prescription to see complete information
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding Prescription Details

Each digital prescription contains comprehensive information to help you understand your treatment.

### What's Included

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Diagnosis" icon="stethoscope">
    The condition or symptoms your doctor is treating
  </Card>
  <Card title="Medications" icon="pills">
    Complete list of prescribed medicines with dosages
  </Card>
  <Card title="Instructions" icon="clipboard-list">
    How to take each medication, frequency, and duration
  </Card>
  <Card title="Doctor Details" icon="user-doctor">
    Name, specialty, and contact information of your physician
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Medication Information

For each medication in your prescription, you'll see:

- **Medication Name** - Generic and brand names (e.g., Paracetamol / Crocin)
- **Strength** - Dosage strength (e.g., 500mg, 10mg)
- **Dosage Instructions** - How much to take and when (e.g., "1 tablet twice daily after meals")
- **Duration** - How long to continue the medication (e.g., "5 days", "2 weeks")
- **Special Instructions** - Any specific warnings or guidance from your doctor

<Tip>
  **Tap on any medication** to see additional information like composition, common uses, and general precautions.
</Tip>

## Taking Prescriptions to the Pharmacy

Aria digital prescriptions are accepted at pharmacies across India.

### At the Pharmacy Counter

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Your Prescription">
    Launch Aria and navigate to the prescription you need to fill
  </Step>
  <Step title="Show the Pharmacist">
    Display your mobile screen to the pharmacist, or use the share feature to send the prescription
  </Step>
  <Step title="QR Code Verification">
    Pharmacists can scan the QR code on your prescription for verification and authenticity
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get Your Medications">
    The pharmacist dispenses your medications based on the digital prescription
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **ABHA-Linked Prescriptions** - Your digital prescriptions are linked to your ABHA health ID, making them verifiable and accepted across the ABDM network.
</Info>

### Download or Print

If you prefer a physical copy:

- Tap the **Download** button to save a PDF version
- Print the PDF at home or at a local print shop
- Share the PDF via email or messaging apps

Stay on top of your treatment with Aria's medication tracking features.

### Active Medications

See all medications you're currently taking:

<Steps>

  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Dosage Schedule">

    Mark doses as taken and monitor your adherence
  </Step>

<Tip>
  **Medication Reminders** - Enable push notifications in the Aria app to receive timely reminders when it's time to take your medications.

### Medication History

Review your complete medication history:

- See all medications you've taken over time
- Identify patterns in your treatments
- Track changes in dosages or medications
- Share comprehensive medication history with new doctors

## Understanding Your Diagnosis

Each prescription includes your diagnosis - the condition your doctor is treating.

### Diagnosis Details Include

- **Primary Diagnosis** - Main condition being treated (e.g., "Acute Upper Respiratory Infection")
- **Symptoms** - Specific symptoms noted by your doctor (e.g., "Fever, cough, sore throat")
- **Clinical Findings** - Any measurements or observations (e.g., "Fever 99.8°F")
- **Treatment Plan** - Brief overview of the treatment approach

<Note>
  **Questions About Your Diagnosis?** - If you need clarification about your diagnosis or treatment, use Aria's messaging feature to contact your doctor, or schedule a follow-up appointment.
</Note>

## Sharing Prescriptions

Easily share your prescriptions with family members, caregivers, or other healthcare providers.

### Share Options

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Prescription">
    Select the prescription you want to share
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Share">
    Click the share icon in the top right corner
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Method">
    Share via WhatsApp, email, text message, or download as PDF
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    The prescription is shared with complete details and verification QR code
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Share prescriptions with family members who pick up medications on your behalf, or with specialists who need to see your current treatments.
</Tip>

## Prescription Refills

Keep track of when you need prescription refills.

### Managing Refills

- Review prescription duration to know when medications will run out
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up appointments before medications end
- Contact your doctor through Aria if you need a refill
- View prescription history to request refills for chronic medications

<Info>
  **Controlled Medications** - Some medications require new prescriptions and cannot be refilled. Check with your doctor about refill policies for your specific medications.
</Info>
