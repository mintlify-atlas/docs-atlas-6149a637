## Share Records Securely

Aria makes it simple to share your medical records with any healthcare provider. Whether you're visiting a new doctor, getting a second opinion, or coordinating care between specialists, you can share the exact information needed with just a few taps.

<Info>
  **You Control Your Data** - You decide what to share, who can see it, and for how long. Share only the records relevant to each healthcare provider.
</Info>

## How to Share Records

Aria offers multiple ways to share your medical information securely.

### Share Individual Records

Share specific prescriptions or documents:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the Record">
    Open the prescription, lab report, or document you want to share
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Share">
    Click the share icon in the top right corner
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Sharing Method">
    Select how you want to share:
    - **Direct share** to another Aria user
    - **WhatsApp** for instant messaging
    - **Email** for email delivery
    - **Download PDF** to save and share manually
    - **Generate Link** for web access
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and Send">
    Review what you're sharing and tap **Send**
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Share Multiple Records

Create a custom collection of records to share:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Share Center">
    Tap **Share** from the main menu
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select Records">
    Choose multiple prescriptions, lab reports, or documents to include
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Recipient">
    Enter the healthcare provider's email or ABHA ID, or select a family member
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Access Duration">
    Choose how long the recipient can access these records (24 hours, 1 week, 1 month, or permanent)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send Package">
    Review your selection and send the complete medical record package
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Create Health Summaries** - For new doctor visits, select your most recent prescriptions, relevant lab reports, and important imaging results to give a comprehensive overview.
</Tip>

## Sharing with Healthcare Providers

Different sharing options for different healthcare scenarios.

### Share via ABHA ID

If the healthcare provider is on the ABDM network:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Request Provider's ABHA ID">
    Ask the doctor or hospital for their ABHA healthcare professional ID
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share Directly">
    In Aria, enter the ABHA ID and select records to share
  </Step>
  <Step title="Instant Access">
    The provider can access your records immediately through the ABDM network
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **ABDM Network** - ABHA-linked sharing ensures your records are accessible across India's national digital health ecosystem, connecting hospitals, clinics, and healthcare providers.
</Info>

### Share via QR Code

For in-person consultations:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate QR Code">
    In the share menu, select **Generate QR Code** for the records you want to share
  </Step>
  <Step title="Show to Provider">
    Display the QR code on your phone screen
  </Step>
  <Step title="Doctor Scans Code">
    The healthcare provider scans the code with their Aria Doctor app or ABDM-compatible system
  </Step>
  <Step title="Instant Access">
    Your selected medical records appear in their system immediately
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  QR codes are time-limited and expire after the consultation for your security. You can regenerate them anytime.
</Note>

### Share via Link

For remote consultations or telemedicine:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Secure Link">
    Select records and choose **Generate Link** from share options
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Access Controls">
    Configure link expiration (1 day to 30 days) and password protection if desired
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy and Send">
    Copy the secure link and send it via email, WhatsApp, or messaging app
  </Step>
  <Step title="Recipient Views Online">
    The healthcare provider clicks the link to view your records in a web browser
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sharing with Family Members

Grant family members or caregivers access to help manage your healthcare.

### Add a Caregiver

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    Tap **Settings** > **Family & Caregivers**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Caregiver">
    Tap **Add Caregiver** and enter their name, phone number, or email
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Permissions">
    Choose what they can access:
    - **View only** - See your records but can't share them
    - **View and share** - Access and share records on your behalf
    - **Full access** - Manage your account including uploading records
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send Invitation">
    The caregiver receives an invitation to access your medical records through Aria
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **For Elderly Parents or Dependents** - Add yourself as a caregiver to their Aria account to help manage appointments, medications, and medical records.
</Tip>

### Manage Caregiver Access

- View all active caregivers in your account
- Modify permissions anytime
- See when caregivers access your records (audit log)
- Revoke access instantly if needed

## What Information is Shared

Understand exactly what's included when you share medical records.

### Prescription Sharing Includes

- **Diagnosis** - The condition being treated
- **Medications** - All prescribed medications with dosages and instructions
- **Doctor Information** - Prescribing physician's name and credentials
- **Date and Time** - When the prescription was created
- **Patient Details** - Your name, age, and ABHA ID (if applicable)

### Lab Report Sharing Includes

- **Test Results** - All lab values and measurements
- **Reference Ranges** - Normal ranges for comparison
- **Test Date** - When the sample was collected and analyzed
- **Laboratory Information** - Name and accreditation of the testing facility
- **Ordering Physician** - Doctor who requested the tests

### Document Sharing Includes

- **Original Document** - High-quality scan or photo
- **Document Metadata** - Date, type, and description
- **Upload Information** - When and by whom it was added to your records

<Note>
  **Selective Sharing** - You can choose to share specific information while keeping other records private. For example, share only relevant prescriptions with a specialist without sharing your entire medical history.
</Note>

## Managing Shared Access

Keep track of who has access to your medical records and manage permissions.

### View Active Shares

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Share Management">
    Go to **Settings** > **Shared Access** or **Privacy** > **Access Log**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review Active Shares">
    See all active shares, who has access, and what records they can view
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Expiration">
    View when each share will expire automatically
  </Step>
  <Step title="Revoke if Needed">
    Tap **Revoke Access** to immediately remove someone's access to your records
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Access Audit Log

Track who has viewed your medical records:

- See a chronological log of all record access
- View which specific records were accessed
- See timestamps and user information
- Identify any unexpected access

<Info>
  **Privacy Protection** - The audit log helps ensure your medical information is only accessed by authorized individuals. Report any suspicious activity to Aria support immediately.
</Info>

### Time-Limited Access

All shares can be set to expire automatically:

- **24 Hours** - For single consultations or emergency access
- **1 Week** - For ongoing treatment or specialist consultations
- **1 Month** - For extended care coordination
- **Permanent** - For trusted caregivers or family members

<Tip>
  **Best Practice** - Use the shortest access duration necessary. You can always extend or re-share if needed.
</Tip>

### Revoke Access Anytime

You can instantly revoke shared access:

- Remove access to specific records
- Revoke all access for a specific person
- Expire links immediately
- Disable QR codes

## Sharing During Emergencies

In emergency situations, quick access to your medical information can be life-saving.

### Emergency Access Card

Set up emergency access for critical information:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Emergency Profile">
    Go to **Settings** > **Emergency Access**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Critical Information">
    Include:
    - Blood type
    - Known allergies
    - Current medications
    - Chronic conditions
    - Emergency contacts
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Lock Screen Access">
    Allow emergency information to be viewed from your phone's lock screen
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add ICE Contacts">
    Designate In Case of Emergency (ICE) contacts who can access full medical records
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **Emergency Access** - First responders and emergency room doctors can access your critical medical information even if you're unconscious or unable to unlock your phone.
</Info>

## Troubleshooting

Common issues and solutions when sharing medical records.

### Recipient Can't Access Shared Records

- Verify the email address or ABHA ID is correct
- Check that the share hasn't expired
- Ensure the recipient has internet connectivity
- Try generating a new share link
- Contact Aria support if issues persist

### Share Link Not Working

- Check if the link has expired
- Verify the link was copied completely
- Try opening in a different browser
- Generate a new link and try again

### QR Code Won't Scan

- Increase screen brightness
- Hold phone steady and ensure QR code is fully visible
- Check that the recipient's scanner is working
- Try generating a new QR code
